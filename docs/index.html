Assumption

How the 50/50 split works

Your short two-night stops are where a literal 50/50 split gets awkward, so this guide treats the whole trip as the unit of balance instead of every single base.

Result: 10 city-focus days and 8 out-of-city days, with transfer days doing some of the work in Hokuriku and Hida.

All transport family totals assume four adult-equivalent fares. Real family cost can be lower if some travelers qualify for child pricing.

Trip Math

How many days are you actually in Japan?

This calculation assumes a direct Brisbane-Narita outbound on Tuesday October 28, 2026 and a direct Narita-Brisbane return leaving Japan on Tuesday November 17, 2026.

Current schedule data shows direct Brisbane → Tokyo flights at about 8h 55m to 9h 10m, which still lands you in Japan on October 28 because Japan is one hour behind Brisbane.

Current schedule data shows direct Tokyo → Brisbane flights at about 8h 50m to 9h 05m, which typically gets you back to Brisbane on Wednesday November 18, 2026 if you fly out of Japan on the evening of November 17.

Result: 20 nights in Japan, 21 calendar dates touched, and 19 full sightseeing days between arrival and departure.

Interpretation

What that means for planning

Your earlier 20-night structure is still the right planning shape. The useful correction is that the trip gives you 19 full sightseeing days, not 20 or 21 fully usable days.

If you choose the Singapore stopover instead, the Japan portion drops to 18 nights and 17 full sightseeing days.

Yayoi Kusama Museum

Best base: Final Tokyo stay

Best slot: Saturday Nov 14, 2026

Budget note: JPY 1,100 / AUD 9.70 adult

Strong fit for the final Tokyo weekend because the museum is open Thursday to Sunday and national holidays only. Tickets are timed, 90 minutes, and online-only.

S32

Oedo Antique Market

Best base: Final Tokyo stay

Best slot: Sunday Nov 15, 2026

Budget note: Free entry

The official Oedo pages say the Tokyo International Forum market runs on the first and third Sundays each month. That means your trip hits Sunday November 1 and Sunday November 15, 2026; November 15 is the cleaner fit because it is inside your final Tokyo block. Market is canceled if it rains.

S34 S40

Nippori Fabric Town

Best base: Final Tokyo stay

Best slot: Saturday Nov 14 or Monday Nov 16, 2026

Budget note: Shopping spend varies

Very easy Tokyo shopping half-day. The official district site says the area covers about 80 shops over roughly one kilometer from Nippori Station.

S35

Koedo Kawagoe day trip

Best base: Final Tokyo stay

Best slot: Monday Nov 16, 2026

Budget note: Rail cost varies by start station

Best placed in the final Tokyo block. The official tourism site pitches Kawagoe as about 30 minutes from the city center, with enough retro streets, snacks, and old-town walking to fill most of a day without overcommitting.

S33

Cup Noodles Museum

Best base: Osaka

Best slot: Nov 2 or Nov 4, 2026

Budget note: Osaka Ikeda entry free; My CUPNOODLES Factory JPY 500 / AUD 4.41 each

Osaka Ikeda is the cleaner fit than Yokohama for this route. The official site says it is about 20 minutes from Hankyu Osaka-Umeda to Ikeda Station, then about a 5-minute walk. Keep Yokohama as a backup only if you later rebalance Tokyo.

S37 S38

Hungry Osaka food walking tour

Best base: Osaka

Best slot: Evening on Nov 2 or Nov 3, 2026

Budget note: JPY 13,000 / AUD 114.58 pp

This is a direct match for your food-first Osaka stop. Official tour details: 3 to 3.5 hours, 15 dishes, 3 drinks, Shinsekai focus, and meeting outside Ebisucho Station Exit 3.

Tokyo | 4 nights

Start with Tokyo for the big-city landing: temples, neon, department-store food halls, parks, and enough easy family logistics to shake off jet lag without rushing into long transfers.

2026-10-28 to 2026-11-01

If 50% of the time stays in the city

  • Asakusa and Senso-ji for a classic first-day Tokyo feel, with a Sumida River or Tokyo Skytree add-on.

Trip Math

How many days are you actually in Japan?

This calculation assumes a direct Brisbane-Narita outbound on Tuesday October 28, 2026 and a direct Narita-Brisbane return leaving Japan on Tuesday November 17, 2026.

Current schedule data shows direct Brisbane → Tokyo flights at about 8h 55m to 9h 10m, which still lands you in Japan on October 28 because Japan is one hour behind Brisbane.

Current schedule data shows direct Tokyo → Brisbane flights at about 8h 50m to 9h 05m, which typically gets you back to Brisbane on Wednesday November 18, 2026 if you fly out of Japan on the evening of November 17.

Result: 20 nights in Japan, 21 calendar dates touched, and 19 full sightseeing days between arrival and departure.

Interpretation

What that means for planning

Your earlier 20-night structure is still the right planning shape. The useful correction is that the trip gives you 19 full sightseeing days, not 20 or 21 fully usable days.

If you choose the Singapore stopover instead, the Japan portion drops to 18 nights and 17 full sightseeing days.
